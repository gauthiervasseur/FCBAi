#### Why This Matters

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Classic.png') -->

# Ukraine has the talent. The U.S. has the scale.

- Ukraine offers **deep agronomy expertise, engineering talent, and real-field validation**.
- The U.S. offers **capital, large commercial farms, strategic partners, and distribution access**.
- The opportunity is to turn **battle-tested solutions** into **globally scalable companies**.

**Key mindset:** the U.S. market will not adapt to you — **you must adapt to it**.

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Classic.png') -->

# The gap you must close

- **Ukraine strength:** technical depth, resilience, speed, cost-efficient innovation
- **U.S. expectation:** scalable business model, clean GTM, ROI proof, investor readiness
- **Your task:** translate your advantage into the language of **U.S. customers and investors**

**Do not rebuild everything. Reposition it.**

---

##### Market Reality

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Right_Classic.png') -->

# The U.S. market is a system, not just a geography

![bg left:25%](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1500937386664-56d1dfef3854?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

## Farmers buy outcomes, not technology.
The buyer is focused on yield, input costs, labor, weather risk, and reliability. Novelty alone does not matter.

## Adoption is slow because trust is earned.
Expect long sales cycles, field proof requirements, and repeated validation before expansion.

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Left_Classic.png') -->

![bg right:20%](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1592982537447-7440770cbfc9?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

# Your core translation: ROI per acre

## Every value proposition must become economics.
Translate impact into one or more of these:
- higher yield
- lower input cost
- labor savings
- lower operational risk

## Your best language is simple.
Use **$ / acre / season**, payback period, and operational reliability.

#### Investor Readiness

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Classic.png') -->

# What U.S. investors actually ask

- Can this scale in the U.S. market?
- Who is the exact buyer?
- Why does this win against existing alternatives?
- What is the ROI and payback logic?
- Can this become a **venture-scale company**?

**Great technology is not enough. A credible growth machine is required.**

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/inverted/Content.png') -->

# What makes you fundable

- **Clear economic value** for the farm or channel partner
- **Paid traction**, not only pilots
- **Repeatable GTM** path into the market
- **Focused wedge**: one customer, one use case, one region first
- **Founder credibility** and ability to execute under constraint

**Clarity beats complexity.**

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Right_Classic.png') -->

# Traction that counts

![bg left:25%](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1523741543316-beb7fc7023d8?auto=format&fit=crop&w=900&q=80)

## Weak signals
Free pilots, generic interest, LOIs without follow-through, users without usage depth

## Strong signals
Paid pilots, retained customers, distributor engagement, repeat usage, first revenue

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Classic.png') -->

# Minimum investor-readiness checklist

| Area      | What needs to be true                                |
| :-------- | :--------------------------------------------------- |
| Structure | Delaware C-Corp or clear U.S. structuring path       |
| Ownership | Clean cap table, founder agreements, IP assignments  |
| Product   | Clear use case and value proposition for U.S. buyer  |
| Market    | Focused customer segment and realistic GTM plan      |
| Evidence  | Paid pilots, early revenue, or repeatable validation |
| Story     | Simple, credible, venture-scale narrative            |

# Your entry options

<div class="columns3">
<div>

## Direct to farmer
Best for high-control learning

Hard to scale early because CAC and trust-building are high.

</div>

<div>

## Distributors / co-ops
Best for faster reach

Works when product fits existing buying behavior and channel incentives.

</div>

<div>

## Strategic partners
Best for credibility

Agribusiness partners can accelerate pilots, validation, and commercial access.

</div>
</div>

**Default advice:** do not enter alone if channel leverage is possible.

---


<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/inverted/Content_2col.png') -->

# Recommended U.S. entry strategy

<div class="columns2">
<div>

## Phase 1
- one region
- one crop or use case
- one target buyer

## Goal
Prove ROI in a narrow wedge.

</div>

<div>

## Phase 2
- convert pilots to paid relationships
- build reference customers
- secure channel or strategic partner

## Goal
Create repeatability before broad expansion.

</div>
</div>

**Focus first. Scale second.**

# Regulatory awareness is part of strategy

- Biologicals and inputs may require **EPA / USDA** pathways
- Food-related products may require **FDA** compliance
- Data-heavy products need trust, documentation, and enterprise-grade handling

<br>

**Do not treat regulation as a late-stage issue.** Build the timeline and cost assumptions early.

---

<!-- _backgroundImage: url('../../bkg/FCBAi/content/classic/Content_Right_Classic.png') -->

- faster iteration under constraint
- deeper agronomic knowledge
- unique field data or validation
- cost-efficient R&D and deployment
- resilience and operating discipline

**“We are from Ukraine” is context. It is not the value proposition.**

1. Define a **specific U.S. use case** and target buyer

3. Secure first U.S. pilot or partner conversations

5. Build an investor-ready story and diligence package

**By Demo Day, you should look market-ready — not exploratory.**
